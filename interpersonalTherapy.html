What is Interpersonal Therapy ?


Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) is a short-term, structured psychotherapy focused on improving interpersonal relationships
and alleviating symptoms of mental health conditions such as depression. IPT operates on the premise that psychological issues
and personal relationships are interconnected. It targets specific problem areas, including unresolved grief, role disputes and
interpersonal deficits. By enhancing interpersonal functioning, IPT aims to reduce emotional distress and improve overall mental health.
It's particularly effective for depression but also used for other mood and anxiety disorders.
